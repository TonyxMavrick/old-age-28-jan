Please read these Terms carefully before using our services. If you do not agree with any part of these Terms, please do not engage our services.

2. Services Provided

GoldenHaven Senior Care provides the following non-medical home care services:

  • Personal Care: Assistance with activities of daily living including bathing, dressing, grooming, and toileting
  • Companionship: Social interaction, conversation, and emotional support
  • Homemaking: Light housekeeping, meal preparation, and laundry
  • Medication Reminders: Reminding clients to take prescribed medications (not administration)
  • Transportation: Accompaniment to appointments, errands, and social activities
  • Respite Care: Temporary relief for family caregivers
  • Specialized Care: Dementia care, mobility assistance, and 24/7 live-in care
Important Notice: GoldenHaven Senior Care is a non-medical home care agency. We do not provide medical services, medical diagnosis, or treatment. For skilled nursing or medical care, please consult with licensed medical professionals.

3. Eligibility & Consent

3.1 Age Requirement

Our services are designed for individuals aged 55 and older, or adults with disabilities requiring assistance.

3.2 Consent

By engaging our services, you confirm that:

  • You have the legal authority to enter into this agreement
  • You have the capacity to consent to services, or a legal guardian has provided consent
  • All information provided during the assessment is accurate and complete

3.3 Right to Refuse Service

We reserve the right to refuse or discontinue service if:

  • The client's needs exceed our non-medical care capabilities
  • The environment poses safety risks to our caregivers
  • There is evidence of abuse, harassment, or threatening behavior
  • Payment obligations are not met

4. Fees & Payment

4.1 Service Rates

Service rates are determined based on the level of care required, frequency of visits, and type of services provided. Detailed pricing will be provided during your free consultation and outlined in your personalized Service Agreement.

4.2 Payment Terms

  • Payment is due upon receipt of invoice unless otherwise agreed
  • Accepted payment methods include check, credit card, and ACH transfer
  • Late payments may incur a fee of 1.5% per month or the maximum allowed by law
  • Services may be suspended for accounts more than 30 days past due

4.3 Minimum Service Requirements

Most services require a minimum booking of 3-4 hours. Live-in care requires minimum weekly commitments.

4.4 Holiday & Weekend Rates

Services provided on recognized holidays and weekends may be subject to premium rates as outlined in your Service Agreement.

We will work with you to create a care schedule that meets your needs. Regular schedules provide consistency and are preferred when possible.

5.2 Cancellation Policy

  • 24-Hour Notice: Cancellations with at least 24 hours' notice will not incur charges

  • Emergency Cancellations: Will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis

5.3 Our Cancellation Rights

We reserve the right to cancel or reschedule services due to:

  • Caregiver illness or emergency

6. Client Responsibilities

6.1 Safe Environment

Clients are responsible for:

  • Providing a safe, clean working environment for caregivers
  • Disclosing any hazards, pets, or environmental concerns
  • Ensuring adequate lighting, heating, and ventilation
  • Providing necessary supplies and equipment for care tasks

6.2 Accurate Information

Clients must:

  • Provide complete and accurate health information
  • Notify us promptly of changes in condition, medications, or care needs
  • Inform us of any incidents or concerns
  • Update emergency contact information as needed

6.3 Respect & Conduct

Clients and their families must treat caregivers with respect and dignity. Harassment, discrimination, or abusive behavior of any kind will not be tolerated and may result in immediate termination of services.

7. Caregiver Standards

7.1 Qualifications

All GoldenHaven caregivers:

  • Complete comprehensive background checks
  • Are bonded and insured
  • Receive ongoing training and supervision
  • Maintain current CPR and First Aid certification
  • Undergo specialized training for dementia and Alzheimer's care

7.2 Code of Conduct

Our caregivers are expected to:

  • Treat all clients with dignity, respect, and compassion
  • Maintain professional boundaries
  • Arrive punctually for scheduled shifts
  • Follow the agreed-upon care plan
  • Report any changes in client condition
  • Maintain confidentiality

7.3 Caregiver Matching

We carefully match caregivers based on skills, personality, and your specific needs. If you are not satisfied with your caregiver match, please contact us immediately so we can make adjustments.

Information may only be disclosed:
